A room of one's own

If you ask me what a woman is, I'll simply say a human like every other. However, if we ask society what a woman is, that's a different discussion. Women, just like any other being, have been stripped of their rights ever since. Women’s sole purpose in this universe is to get married and serve their husbands. Studying and being able to speak here in front of you all is a privilege to me, thanks to the women who have come before me.Virginia Woolf is a writer and considered one of the most important modernist 20th-century authors. In her essay "A Room of One's Own," she depicts the struggles of women in the literary world and in society in general. According to Woolf, women are limited. We are limited to writing, dreaming, and being ourselves. They are limited by financial struggles, space, and society's expectations. These unequal opportunities for women make them like objects or pets; they are forced to cling to something or someone who treats them as if they’re not human beings. This speech is a tribute to those women who have come before me and paved the way for us.
